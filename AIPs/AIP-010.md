## Abstract

AIP-010 establishes Arrow's first unified tokenomics framework. $ARROW supply is fixed at 100,000,000 with no new minting; all token deployment draws from the existing DAO treasury. The token has three active utilities in Phase 1 (2026): governance voting, slashable manufacturing bonds ([AIP-009](AIP-009.md)), and contributor compensation ([AIP-004](AIP-004.md)). Treasury deployment is governed by an annual cap that the DAO must approve by governance vote each year. The proposed Year 1 (2026) cap is 5,000,000 $ARROW across three buckets: 1,500,000 for a contributor grants program, 500,000 for manufacturing rewards, and 3,000,000 for long-term engineering project funding, which the DAO may allocate to projects directly or convert to USDC through individually voted sales. Phase 2 and 3 mechanics (operator staking, network usage rewards) are described as a non-binding roadmap, with parameters deferred to future AIPs. $ARROW does not entitle holders to profit or dividends.

## Motivation

Arrow has operated for over three years without a unified tokenomics framework, and roughly 76.5M $ARROW (~76% of supply) sits in the DAO treasury with no formal plan. Three things make a framework necessary now:

1. **AIP-009 is live.** Manufacturer bonds are the first protocol-native token utility. Without a broader framework, this mechanic exists in isolation: there is no documented relationship between bonds, the treasury, and contributor compensation.
2. **The DAO funds multi-year engineering projects from a treasury that is mostly $ARROW.** The DAO's liquid USDC reserves are nearly depleted, while five active projects carry approved monthly budget caps. Without a governance-controlled path to deploy or sell treasury $ARROW, operations depend entirely on product revenue and new OTC inflows.
3. **Bounty-first work demands a token that means something.** [AIP-004](AIP-004.md) requires ≥10% $ARROW in all grants. That compensation is only motivating if contributors trust there is a path to liquidity and utility.

The original token allocation earmarked 20% for early contributors, 20% for commitment track and Coordinape compensation, 10% for future investors, and 50% as "TBD / use in Arrow rideshare protocol." That 50% block, sitting largely undisturbed in the treasury, is what this AIP begins to fill in.

## Specification

The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "MAY", "SHOULD", and "SHOULD NOT" in this document are to be interpreted as described in [RFC 2119](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc2119).

$ARROW has three active utilities under this framework: **governance** (§3), **manufacturing bonds** (§4), and **contributor compensation** (§6). All other token mechanics are future roadmap.

### 1. Token Supply

Total supply is fixed at **100,000,000 $ARROW**. New tokens MUST NOT be minted under this framework; all deployment described in this AIP draws from the existing DAO treasury balance. Future dilutive minting MUST NOT proceed without a standalone governance AIP with explicit community approval.

### 3. Governance

$ARROW functions as a governance token. Holders have no entitlement to profit, dividends, or distributions; value derives from the utility of governing the network.

- 1 $ARROW = 1 vote on Snapshot (arrowair.eth).
- Major protocol decisions, treasury allocations, and project approvals MUST be decided by token-weighted vote.
- The GBC ([AIP-002](AIP-002.md)) continues to administer grants and bounties under DAO oversight.
- The Projects Framework ([AIP-006](AIP-006.md)) governs project funding and accountability.

A long-term goal is to migrate all governance on-chain, eliminating Snapshot and the treasury multisig. That transition is deferred to a future AIP and is not normative under V1.

### 4. Manufacturing Bonds

The manufacturing bond mechanism is defined in [AIP-009](AIP-009.md). This AIP formalizes its relationship to the broader tokenomics framework.

- **Enrollment:** a manufacturer MUST apply to the DAO, which MUST issue a risk-adjusted bond limit before the manufacturer may fulfill orders.
- **Fulfillment:** to fulfill an order, a manufacturer MUST have sufficient unbonded $ARROW. Each catalog item specifies an $ARROW bond amount per unit shipped; the DAO maintains the catalog and MAY adjust bond amounts and commission rates via governance.
- **Locking:** when a unit ships, the corresponding $ARROW MUST be locked on the manufacturer's bond for 28 days while the customer receives and inspects the product. After 28 days with no active dispute, locked tokens MUST be released.
- **Slashing:** if the DAO upholds a customer dispute, it MAY slash a portion of the bond. Slashed tokens MUST first compensate the affected customer up to the full order value; any excess MUST be burned permanently, reducing total supply. The DAO MUST NOT retain slashed tokens as revenue.
- **Unstaking cooldown:** 28 days, aligned with the per-unit lock period.
- **DAO commission:** each sale pays the DAO treasury a per-item commission percentage set in the catalog, varying by Arrow's design investment in the product.
- **Rewards:** manufacturers earn $ARROW rewards proportional to sales volume or targeted DAO incentives, funded from the manufacturing rewards bucket in §5.
- **Arbitration:** the DAO MUST serve as final arbiter for customer disputes and slashing decisions.

### 5. Annual Treasury Deployment Cap

The DAO MUST authorize an annual $ARROW deployment cap via governance vote each year, specifying the total cap and bucket allocation. Deployment is not automatic and MUST NOT proceed without this authorization. Undeployed $ARROW remains in the treasury with no automatic rollover.

**Year 1 (2026) proposed cap: 5,000,000 $ARROW** (~6.5% of the treasury balance; ~$1.5M notional at the $0.30 reference price).

| Bucket | $ARROW | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Grants program | 1,500,000 | Contributor grants and bounties (~125K/month). Administered by the GBC initially; MAY migrate to a dedicated DAO bounty board or governance app. Also the sandbox for token-incentive experiments (§6). |
| Manufacturing rewards | 500,000 | Seed pool for AIP-009 bonded manufacturers, proportional to sales volume or targeted DAO incentives. |
| Project funding | 3,000,000 | Funding for long-term engineering projects. Projects MAY receive $ARROW directly, or the DAO MAY convert tokens to USDC to finance approved project budgets (see below). |
| Operator incentives | 0 | Phase 2 — activates when operator staking goes live (see Roadmap). |
| **Total** | **5,000,000** | Authorized by the annual cap vote. |

**Project funding sales.** The project funding bucket authorizes the *purpose*, not the transactions. Each conversion of $ARROW to USDC — whether OTC or on-market — MUST be approved by its own governance vote covering the mechanics of the transaction: amount, venue, counterparty, and pricing. Sales from this bucket are treasury financing for approved engineering work, not speculation; this bucket exists to give the DAO a governance-authorized way to convert treasury $ARROW into operating capital without depending on further OTC rounds.

**Future years.** Bucket allocations SHOULD be revisited annually alongside the cap vote. As the manufacturing protocol matures and operator staking comes online, the operator incentives bucket activates and other proportions MAY be adjusted.

Treasury deployment exists to fund real protocol actors — contributors completing work, manufacturers shipping products, projects building aircraft — and MUST NOT be used to support speculation or create artificial token demand.

### 6. Contributor Compensation

Per [AIP-004](AIP-004.md), every grant or bounty MUST include ≥10% of its value in $ARROW, priced at the $0.30 reference with a 1.5× multiplier. Contributor $ARROW is issued through vesting contracts (ArrowVestingFactory on Optimism); on cancellation, vested tokens go to the contributor and unvested tokens return to the treasury.

Stream-vs-bounty structure remains at each project lead's discretion under [AIP-006](AIP-006.md) — this AIP does not mandate stream deprecation. New token-denominated incentives (for example, an enhanced multiplier for 100% $ARROW bounties) SHOULD be trialed as experiments within the grants program (§5) before any change to AIP-004.

### 7. Liquidity

$ARROW compensation and the bounty-first model depend on contributors having a path to convert $ARROW to USDC. In V1, the DAO's authorized market activity is the sale of project funding tokens under the per-sale vote requirements of §5, sized and paced to available liquidity. A proof-of-concept $ARROW-USDC pool exists on Uniswap V3 (Ethereum Mainnet) but is too thin to support size.

Creating or funding a **DAO-owned liquidity pool** is a more consequential action: it MUST be proposed as a separate governance action and SHOULD follow a formal legal review of the DAO's structure and securities exposure. It is not authorized by this AIP.

## Roadmap (Non-Binding)

The following describes intended direction. Nothing in this section is binding; each mechanism requires its own future AIP with parameters informed by market conditions at the time.

| Phase | Timeframe | Product Milestone | Token Mechanics Active |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Phase 1 | 2026 | Manufacturing protocol live, Quiver dev kits shipping, bounty-first projects operational | Manufacturing bonds, contributor vesting, authorized $ARROW sales for project funding, grants program, 1.5× multiplier |
| Phase 2 | 2027+ | Cargo network launch with Spearhead aircraft and initial vertiports, cargo drone certification, larger drone development | Vertiport operator staking, aircraft operator staking, operator vesting contracts, operator incentives bucket active |
| Phase 3 | 2028+ | Manned aircraft, early air taxi platform | Per-flight network rewards, loyalty rebates, mature staking ecosystem, on-chain governance |

**Vertiport operator staking (Phase 2).** Operators post $ARROW stake to register a vertiport in the Arrow network. Protocol fee discounts follow an exponential decay model — more $ARROW staked means a lower per-flight protocol fee — so small rural operators stake less at a higher base rate while large hubs can justify larger stakes. Registration incentives are geographically weighted toward underserved corridors, with a tapering onboarding budget that decays with each additional registration in a metro. Operators receive $ARROW via vesting-style contracts tied to compliant operation, and poorly performing vertiports may face stake slashing via DAO arbitration. The decay model and geographic weighting are explicitly designed to prevent large operators from dominating the network with pure capital: Arrow's mission is a shared network of independent operators. Stake parameters are deferred to a dedicated Phase 2 AIP — the MOSAIC eVTOL market and FAA certification timelines are too uncertain to set responsible figures today.

**Aircraft operator staking (Phase 2).** Operators register aircraft on-chain with stake proportional to aircraft value and operational risk profile; maintenance compliance and uptime are tracked on-chain. Specifics will be formalized once Arrow has aircraft approaching commercial certification.

**Network usage rewards (Phase 3).** Per-flight $ARROW rebates to early passengers and cargo customers, tapering as organic demand matures, to bootstrap network effects at platform launch. In the same horizon, the DAO may consider investing treasury funds in ecosystem companies such as vertiport or aircraft operators; any such investment would be a separate governance action. The treasury also diversifies from $ARROW-heavy toward a balanced portfolio as the token becomes liquid and protocol revenue grows.

## Rationale

- **Fixed supply, no minting:** protects holders from dilution, simplifies the legal picture, and forces a deliberate, standalone decision if supply ever needs to change.
- **Slashing compensates then burns:** the DAO should not profit from punishing manufacturers; making customers whole comes first and burning the excess keeps incentives clean.
- **Annual cap with buckets:** deployment is deliberate, not passive drawdown. Governance affirmatively decides what $ARROW is for each year. The 3M project funding bucket exists because engineering projects are the DAO's largest expense and the treasury's $ARROW should be usable to fund them.
- **Per-sale votes for treasury conversions:** selling treasury tokens affects every holder; the community approves each transaction's mechanics on the record.
- **Liquidity before LP:** in V1 the DAO funds operations through authorized sales, not treasury LP deployment. A DAO-owned LP remains a reasonable long-term target (fee revenue accrues to the treasury, liquidity is community-controlled) but is a separate governance action requiring legal review.
- **Operator staking deferred:** fiscal responsibility over false precision. The staking mechanisms are established in design; the numbers come later with real market data.
- **No stream mandate:** project leads keep staffing autonomy per AIP-006; incentives toward bounty-first work come from the grants program, not mandates.

## Backwards Compatibility

This AIP is additive. It does not supersede or modify the normative content of any prior AIP.

- [AIP-004](AIP-004.md) (≥10% $ARROW, 1.5× multiplier, $0.30 reference) remains in full effect. An enhanced multiplier for fully $ARROW-denominated bounties is not part of this AIP; it is deferred to grants program experimentation, which will define its own relationship to AIP-004.
- [AIP-009](AIP-009.md) bond mechanics remain in full effect; this AIP adds treasury context and the formal relationship between bonds, rewards, and compensation.
- Deployment buckets operate within the [AIP-006](AIP-006.md)/[AIP-007](AIP-007.md) project budget framework; the grants program cap should be reflected in AIP-007.
- No on-chain contracts are modified. The ArrowVestingFactory and LlamaPay contracts already in use are unchanged.

## Security Considerations

The treasury multisig retains execution authority until on-chain governance ships; it MUST only execute deployments and sales that governance has approved. Market sales of treasury $ARROW should be sized to available liquidity to avoid harming contributors who rely on the pool to convert compensation. The DAO intends to obtain a full legal review of its structure before taking on new categories of treasury action such as a DAO-owned LP.
