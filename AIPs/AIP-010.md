## Abstract

AIP-010 establishes Arrow's first unified tokenomics framework. $ARROW supply is fixed at 100,000,000 with no new minting; all token deployment draws from the existing DAO treasury. The token has three active utilities: governance voting, slashable manufacturing bonds ([AIP-009](AIP-009.md)), and contributor compensation ([AIP-004](AIP-004.md)). Treasury deployment is governed by an annual cap that the DAO must approve by governance vote each year. The proposed Year 1 (2026) cap is 5,000,000 $ARROW across three buckets: 1,500,000 for a grants program, 500,000 for manufacturing rewards, and 3,000,000 for long-term engineering project funding, which the DAO is authorized to deploy directly to projects or convert to USDC through individually voted sales. Operator staking and network rewards are described as a non-binding roadmap, with parameters deferred to future AIPs.

## Motivation

Arrow has operated for over three years without a unified tokenomics framework, and roughly 76.5M $ARROW (~76% of supply) sits in the DAO treasury with no formal plan. Three things make a framework necessary now:

1. **AIP-009 is live.** Manufacturer bonds are the first protocol-native token utility. They need a documented relationship to the treasury, contributor compensation, and future utility.
2. **The DAO funds multi-year engineering projects from a treasury that is mostly $ARROW.** Without a governance-controlled path to deploy or sell treasury tokens, project funding depends entirely on the DAO's finite USDC reserves.
3. **Bounty-first work demands a token that means something.** AIP-004 requires ≥10% $ARROW in all grants. That compensation is only motivating if contributors trust there is a path to liquidity and utility.

The original token allocation earmarked 50% of supply as "TBD / use in Arrow rideshare protocol." This AIP begins to fill that in.

## Specification

The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "MAY", and "SHOULD" in this document are to be interpreted as described in [RFC 2119](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc2119).

### 1. Fixed Supply

Total supply is fixed at **100,000,000 $ARROW**. No new tokens will be minted under this framework; all deployment described here draws from the existing DAO treasury. Any future dilutive minting MUST be proposed as its own standalone AIP.

### 2. Token Utility

**Governance.** 1 $ARROW = 1 vote. All AIPs, treasury allocations, and project approvals are decided by token-weighted vote on Snapshot (arrowair.eth). Long term, the DAO intends to migrate governance fully on-chain. $ARROW confers no entitlement to profit, dividends, or distributions; its value derives from utility.

**Manufacturing bonds (AIP-009).** Manufacturers post slashable $ARROW collateral to sell Arrow products. Each catalog item specifies a per-unit bond that locks for 28 days after shipment while the customer inspects the product. If the DAO upholds a customer dispute, the bond MAY be slashed: slashed tokens compensate the affected customer first, and any excess is **burned permanently**. The DAO does not retain slashed tokens, and burns make the fixed supply modestly deflationary. Each sale also pays the DAO treasury a per-item commission set in the catalog. Manufacturer rewards are funded from the deployment cap in §3.

**Contributor compensation (AIP-004).** Every grant or bounty MUST include ≥10% of its value in $ARROW, priced at the $0.30 reference with a 1.5× multiplier. Contributor $ARROW is issued through vesting contracts (ArrowVestingFactory on Optimism); on cancellation, vested tokens go to the contributor and unvested tokens return to the treasury. Stream-vs-bounty structure remains at each project lead's discretion under [AIP-006](AIP-006.md) — this AIP does not mandate stream deprecation. New token-denominated incentives (for example, an enhanced multiplier for 100% $ARROW bounties) SHOULD be trialed as experiments within the grants program (§3) before any change to AIP-004.

### 3. Annual Treasury Deployment Cap

The DAO authorizes an **annual $ARROW deployment cap** by governance vote each year. Deployment is not automatic: the community affirmatively approves how much $ARROW may leave the treasury and into which buckets. Undeployed $ARROW simply remains in the treasury.

**Year 1 (2026) proposed cap: 5,000,000 $ARROW** (~6.5% of treasury; ~$1.5M notional at the $0.30 reference price).

| Bucket | $ARROW | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Grants program | 1,500,000 | Contributor grants and bounties (~125K/month). Administered by the GBC initially; MAY migrate to a dedicated DAO bounty board or governance app. Also the sandbox for token-incentive experiments (§2). |
| Manufacturing rewards | 500,000 | Seed pool for AIP-009 bonded manufacturers, proportional to sales volume or targeted DAO incentives. |
| Project funding | 3,000,000 | Funding for long-term engineering projects. Projects MAY receive $ARROW directly, or the DAO MAY convert tokens to USDC for project budgets (see below). |
| Operator incentives | 0 | Phase 2 — activates when operator staking goes live (see Roadmap). |
| **Total** | **5,000,000** | Authorized by the annual cap vote. |

**Project funding sales.** The 3M project funding bucket authorizes the *purpose*, not the transactions. Each conversion of $ARROW to USDC — whether OTC or on-market — MUST be approved by its own governance vote covering the mechanics of the transaction: amount, venue, counterparty, and pricing. Buckets are revisited annually alongside the cap vote.

Treasury deployment exists to fund real protocol actors — contributors completing work, manufacturers shipping products, projects building aircraft — not to support speculation or create artificial token demand.

### 4. Liquidity

The DAO is expected to sell a portion of the authorized project funding tokens on the market as liquidity allows, per the vote requirements in §3. Creating or funding a **DAO-owned liquidity pool** is a more consequential action: it MUST be proposed as a separate governance action and SHOULD follow a formal legal review of the DAO's structure and securities exposure.

## Roadmap (Non-Binding)

The following describes intended direction. Nothing in this section is binding; each mechanism requires its own future AIP with parameters informed by market conditions at the time.

**Phase 1 — 2026.** Manufacturing protocol live, Quiver dev kits shipping, first bonded manufacturer onboarded, annual cap vote executed, grants program funded and communicated to the GBC.

**Phase 2 — 2027+.** Start of the cargo network with Spearhead aircraft and early vertiports. **Vertiport operator staking:** operators stake $ARROW to register in the network, with protocol fee discounts on an exponential decay curve (larger stakes earn lower per-flight fees), geographic incentive weighting toward underserved corridors, and slashing for poorly performing operators — a design intended to favor a shared network of independent operators over consolidation. **Aircraft operator staking:** stake proportional to aircraft value and risk profile, with on-chain maintenance compliance. All stake parameters are deferred to dedicated Phase 2 AIPs.

**Phase 3 — 2028+.** Manned aircraft and early air taxi platform. Per-flight $ARROW rewards and loyalty rebates to bootstrap network effects. Treasury diversifies from $ARROW-heavy toward a balanced portfolio, potentially including investments in ecosystem companies such as vertiport and aircraft operators. Full on-chain governance replaces Snapshot and the multisig.

## Rationale

- **Fixed supply, no minting:** protects holders from dilution, simplifies the legal picture, and forces a deliberate, standalone decision if supply ever needs to change.
- **Slashing compensates then burns:** the DAO should not profit from punishing manufacturers; making customers whole comes first and burning the excess keeps incentives clean.
- **Annual cap with buckets:** conservative, predictable deployment that preserves the treasury while giving each token sink a clear budget. The 3M project funding bucket exists because engineering projects are the DAO's largest expense and the treasury's $ARROW should be usable to fund them — under per-transaction governance control.
- **Per-sale votes for treasury conversions:** selling treasury tokens affects every holder; the community should approve each transaction's mechanics on the record.
- **Operator staking deferred:** the MOSAIC eVTOL market and FAA certification timelines are too uncertain to set responsible stake parameters today. Fiscal responsibility over false precision.
- **No stream mandate:** project leads keep staffing autonomy per AIP-006; incentives toward bounty-first work come from the grants program, not mandates.

## Backwards Compatibility

This AIP amends no existing AIP. [AIP-004](AIP-004.md) compensation rules (≥10% $ARROW, 1.5× multiplier, $0.30 reference) remain in force; any change would follow a successful grants program experiment and its own AIP. [AIP-009](AIP-009.md) bond mechanics are formalized, not modified. Deployment buckets operate within the [AIP-006](AIP-006.md)/[AIP-007](AIP-007.md) project budget framework, and the grants program cap should be reflected in AIP-007.

## Security Considerations

The treasury multisig retains execution authority until on-chain governance ships; it MUST only execute deployments and sales that governance has approved. Market sales of treasury $ARROW should be sized to available liquidity to avoid harming contributors who rely on the pool to convert compensation. The DAO intends to obtain a full legal review of its structure before taking on new categories of treasury action such as a DAO-owned LP.
